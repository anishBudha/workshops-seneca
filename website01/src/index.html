    • The Suicide Forest of Japan — Aokigahara

    Do you always include a horror movie in your idea of a relaxing evening? Is Halloween really the only holiday you care about? Is the thought of lying around on a beach boring you to tears? If you said "Hell, yeah!" you're a prime candidate for a horror-themed vacation. There are numerous famously spooky locations where you can go for a quick weekend getaway or a full-on horror tour. And our today's prime location is "The Aokigahara Forest".

    Address: Aokigahara, Fujikawaguchiko, Minamitsuru District, Yamanashi, Japan

    About two hours' drive west of Tokyo is the Yamanashi Prefecture, where the deep forest of Aokigahara may be found near the base of Mount Fuji. With numerous suicides occurring there each year, the forest has long been recognised as the top suicide location in Japan. Japanese officials in the Yamanashi prefecture started recruiting individuals to patrol the area in 2009 as a result of the high number of suicides that were being recorded in the forest.

    A Japanese sign at the forest's entrance serves as a reminder to visitors of the value and rarity of life. A sign in Japanese letters reads, "Please don't suffer alone, and first reach out. Quietly ponder once more about your parents, siblings, or children." The phone number for a suicide hotline is listed at the bottom of the signage. The lush verdant forest is characterised by twisted trees, tunnels, moss-covered roots, and uneven ground in a setting that is eerily silent, which makes it very unsettling. Because of its numerous, dense trees that resemble the sea, the forest is often referred to as "Sea of Trees" (or Jukay in Japanese). Aokigahara Forest is an extremely simple place to get lost due to the dense vegetation. Furthermore, the GPS does not function properly because the high iron content of the soil interferes with cell phone signals. As a result, visitors are strongly advised to stay on the trails.

    But why has Aokigahara grown to be such a well-liked suicide location? There are several causes for this. According to a local tradition, Aokigahara was formerly a location where people engaged in the ancient Japanese habit known as ubasute, which involved transporting elderly or ill individuals to isolated locations and leaving them there to pass away. Another legend claims that during famines, poor people would leave their young children or old relatives in the forest because they were unable to provide for them.

    The evil reputation of Aokigahara has existed for many years. Thanks to the release of numerous books and films about woods, the appeal of the forests among tourists soared. Seich Matsumoto's Tower of Waves, published in 1960, has a protagonist who kills herself at Aokigahara. Aokigahara is described as the ideal spot to die in Wataru Tsurumi's book The Complete Manual of Suicide. More recently, Aokigahara was portrayed in the 2016 horror film "The Forest" from the United States. In the film, a woman ventures into the Japanese bush in an effort to locate her missing twin sister.

    In terms of dark tourism, the forest is exceptional. The warning signs erected around the forest, which are intended to encourage people to view life as a gift and to seek assistance, demonstrate that the local focus is on present tragedy and the mental health issue. Aokigahara is a popular destination for visitors and travellers, including individuals who go there intentionally to commit suicide. In addition to those who visit the forest with the intention of ending their lives, there are also those who go there out of curiosity, as a place for quiet self-reflection, or to mourn the suicides of loved ones. In Margee Kerr's book Scream: Chilling Adventures in the Science of Fear, she gives the example of dark visitors in Aokigahara. The author of the book reflects on her time spent as a visitor in the forest and discusses it in detail. By going to this website, Kerr was able to interact intimately with death, satiating her curiosity about it and discovering the purpose of life.

    Aokigahara is much more than just a dark tourist destination, even if it still attracts them. There are several lava caverns in this area, and there is a plethora of wildlife. Additionally, tourists can take stunning views of Mt. Fuji from that location. Local authorities have avoided disclosing the number of suicides that take place in the woods since 2011 in an effort to draw attention to the forest's natural beauty and decrease its reputation for being a desolate place. In 2010, Vice released a 20-minute documentary about the forest in which geologist Azusa Hayano takes a trip to the Aokigahara Forest where he talks about the history behind it and what he does as an environmentalist to help guide as many individuals as possible not to attempt to commit suicide there.
